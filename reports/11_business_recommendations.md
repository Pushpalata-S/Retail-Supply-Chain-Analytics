Example Business Recommendations

1. Focus on High-Performing Categories

Finding (SQL):

Category-wise sales analysis showed that one category contributes the highest revenue.

Recommendation:

Increase inventory and marketing investment for the top-performing category to maximize revenue.

2. Improve Low-Profit Products

Finding (SQL):

Product analysis identified products with consistently low or negative profits.

Recommendation:

Review pricing, supplier costs, or discontinue products that consistently reduce profitability.

3. Strengthen Customer Retention

Finding (SQL):

Top customers contribute a significant share of revenue.

Recommendation:

Launch loyalty programs and personalized promotions for high-value customers to improve retention.

4. Expand in High-Revenue Regions

Finding (SQL):

Regional analysis identified the highest-performing regions.

Recommendation:

Increase marketing and warehouse capacity in high-performing regions to capitalize on demand.

5. Improve Underperforming Regions

Finding (SQL):

Some regions generate low sales or low profits.

Recommendation:

Investigate local pricing, logistics, and product availability to improve regional performance.

6. Optimize Discount Strategy

Finding (SQL):

High-discount products often have lower profit margins.

Recommendation:

Review discount policies and limit excessive discounts on already low-margin products.

7. Reduce Shipping Time

Finding (Python + SQL):

Feature engineering created shipping_days, and SQL analyzed average shipping time by region.

Recommendation:

Improve logistics in regions with longer delivery times to enhance customer satisfaction.

8. Reduce Return Rate

Finding (SQL):

Return analysis identified the overall return percentage.

Recommendation:

Analyze frequently returned products to identify quality issues or misleading product descriptions.

9. Plan Inventory Around Seasonal Demand

Finding (SQL):

Monthly and quarterly sales analysis revealed seasonal peaks.

Recommendation:

Increase inventory before peak sales periods to prevent stock shortages and reduce excess inventory during slower months.

10. Focus on High-Profit Categories

Finding (SQL):

Category profit analysis showed which categories deliver the highest margins.

Recommendation:

Prioritize marketing and inventory investment in high-profit categories rather than only high-sales categories.
